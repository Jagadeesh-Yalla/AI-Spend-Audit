# Metrics

## North Star Metric

**Credex consultations booked per week**

Why: This is the only metric that directly maps to Credex revenue. DAU/MAU is meaningless for a tool people use once a quarter. Audits completed is a leading indicator but not the goal. Consultations booked = real business value created.

## 3 Input Metrics That Drive the North Star

1. **Audit completion rate** (audits completed / landing page visitors)
   - Target: >25%
   - Why it matters: Low completion = form is too long or confusing

2. **High-savings audit rate** (audits showing >$500/mo savings / total audits)
   - Target: >15%
   - Why it matters: Only high-savings audits trigger the Credex CTA

3. **Email capture rate on high-savings results** (emails captured / high-savings audits shown)
   - Target: >30%
   - Why it matters: Email is the bridge between audit and consultation

## What to Instrument First

1. Landing page → audit page click-through rate
2. Audit form completion rate (started vs submitted)
3. Which tools are most commonly selected (product insight)
4. Results page time-on-page (are people reading the breakdown?)
5. Email capture conversion on results page

## Pivot Trigger

If after 500 audits:
- Consultation booking rate < 1% → the audit isn't surfacing compelling enough savings, or the Credex CTA is weak
- Email capture rate < 5% → results aren't valuable enough, or trust is missing
- Audit completion rate < 15% → form is too long, simplify to 3 tools max

**The number that triggers a pivot: 0 consultation bookings after 200 high-savings audits.**
That means the product-to-sales handoff is broken, not the product itself.
